About Us

Welcome to our Oceanic Buoy Data Project website. This platform is dedicated to providing real-time and historical data collected from an oceanic buoy deployed in the Atlantic Ocean off the shore of Leix√µes (Porto). Our project is a collaborative effort undertaken by students from the Masters in Electrical and Computer Engineering and the Masters in Physics Engineering programs in the curricular unit of Systems Engineering.

Our buoy is equipped with sensors that continuously monitor various oceanographic parameters such as temperature, water temprature, humidity, wave height. The data collected is transmitted in real-time to our servers, allowing us to provide up-to-date information to researchers, students, and the general public.

In addition to live data, our website also offers access to past data, enabling users to analyze trends and patterns over time. This feature is particularly useful for academic research and educational purposes.

The development of this project is part of our curricular unit, where we apply theoretical knowledge and practical skills gained during our courses. Our goal is to enhance our understanding of oceanographic data collection and analysis, while also contributing valuable insights to the scientific community.

Thank you for visiting our site. We hope you find the data and information provided here useful and informative.
